SESSIONS ATTENDED

March 7
  - Keynote: "Infrastructure Resilience in the Modern District" - decent overview, nothing new
  - "Wireless Density in High-Occupancy Environments" - useful. 802.11ax rollout timeline
    is more realistic than I expected. 2020-2021 for most district budgets.
  - "Vendor Panel: Next-Gen Firewall in K-12" - sales pitch disguised as a panel. skipped out
    at the break.

March 8
  - "Segmentation Strategies for Small IT Teams" - best session of the conference. The
    presenter (from a district in Shreveport) has done exactly what I'd recommend for PRISD.
    Got her card - R. Thibodeaux, Caddo Parish. Following up in April.
  - "Google Workspace vs. O365: A 3-Year Review" - not my problem but some of the network
    load data was interesting. Guest WiFi gets hammered during Chromebook deployments.
  - "OSINT Awareness for School Administrators" - this is why I keep coming. Good session.
    Walked through what you can find on a district from public records alone. Most admins
    in the room had no idea their infrastructure was this visible. Will reference this
    for the PRISD engagement.

March 9
  - "Physical Security Integration with Network Access Control" - half good, half product demo.
    The NAC piece was worth staying for.
